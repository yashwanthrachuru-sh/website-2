# EduNet User Manual

Welcome to EduNet! This manual guides you through using all the learning, career building, and assessment features of the EduNet platform.

---

## 🔐 1. Access Control & Registration

### Creating an Account
1.  Navigate to the EduNet home landing page.
2.  Click **Sign Up** on the top navigation bar.
3.  Enter your desired Username, Email Address, Password, and your current Academic branch (e.g. SDE, CSE, ECE).
4.  Click **Register**.
5.  *Note: To prevent unauthorized registrations, all new accounts are set to pending status. An administrator must approve your account before you can log in.*

### Logging In
1.  Click **Login** on the home page.
2.  Enter your Username and Password.
3.  Upon success, you will be redirected to your personal **Student Dashboard**.

---

## 📊 2. Student Dashboard Overview

The dashboard serves as your personal learning workspace. Here you can track:
*   **XP points**: Earned by reading lesson notes, submitting code, and finishing quizzes.
*   **Streak 🔥**: Increments each day you log in and complete a learning activity.
*   **Topic Performance**: Highlights topics where you are strong or need improvement.
*   **Weekly XP Trend**: Visual Chart.js graph tracking your XP growth over the past week.
*   **Learning Heatmap**: A Github-style grid showing your login and study activity over the last 52 weeks.

---

## 🗺 3. Career Roadmaps

1.  Select **Roadmaps** from the sidebar.
2.  Choose a roadmap path corresponding to your target career (e.g., *Frontend Developer*, *Software Development Engineer (SDE)*, *AI/ML Engineer*).
3.  Click **Start Roadmap**.
4.  Navigate through the course modules. Expand a module to view individual lessons.
5.  Each lesson contains structured lecture notes, recommended videos, exercises, and quizzes. Mark milestones to gain XP.

---

## 🧠 4. Interactive Quizzes & Assessments

*   Navigate to **Quizzes** to test your knowledge.
*   Filter quizzes by category: **MCQ** (Multiple Choice Questions), **Aptitude**, or **Coding**.
*   Click **Start Quiz**. Timed assessments must be completed within the allotted duration.
*   Upon submission, view your immediate score, breakdown of correct answers, and gain XP based on your score.
*   View your historic attempts in the **Recent Attempts** table at the bottom of the page.

---

## 💻 5. Browser Coding Lab

1.  Click **Coding Lab** on the sidebar.
2.  Choose a language template (e.g. JavaScript, Python, C++, Java, SQL, or HTML).
3.  Write code inside the editor pane.
4.  Select a practice challenge problem from the left panel.
5.  Click **Run Code** to execute your script in the compiler sandbox and inspect stdout.
6.  Click **Submit** to run unit tests. Passing submissions award XP points.

---

## 📄 6. ATS Resume Builder & Portfolios

### Building your Resume
1.  Click **Resume Builder** on the sidebar.
2.  Fill in your details (Personal Info, Education, Experience, Skills, Projects).
3.  Choose a layout template (e.g., Standard, ATS-Friendly, Modern).
4.  Click **Save Resume** to store your profile.
5.  Use **Download Resume** to export your CV as an ATS-compatible PDF file.

### Syncing GitHub & Portfolio
1.  Navigate to **Settings** or **Profile**.
2.  Connect your **GitHub account** to import repository links automatically.
3.  Pin your best repositories to display them on your public profile.
4.  Recruiters can access your public portfolio URL (e.g., `/portfolio/john_doe`) to inspect your achievements, projects, certificates, and GitHub activity timeline.
